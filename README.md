### What I'm building

**[CloutFarm](https://cloutfarm.ai)** — A marketing agency platform where AI agents and human expertise produce content together. Three production engines (LinkedIn Studio, TikTok/Instagram, Serialized Stories), each with a different service model — from high-touch collaborative writing to fully automated SEO pipelines. The core thesis: every customer interaction deepens a shared knowledge layer, and every engine's output gets better because of it. Strategy conversations, interview transcripts, draft edits, performance data — all feed back into the system. The flywheel is the product.

**[ConvoSearch](https://convosearch.com)** — A Shopify app that replaces catalog browsing with conversational product discovery. Customers describe what they need; the app understands intent, searches inventory, and guides the purchase — no filters, no dropdowns, just dialogue.

### What I think about

**Nuclear energy.** A ~$50B industry bottlenecked almost entirely by capex. The physics works. The fuel is abundant. The waste volume is negligible compared to any fossil alternative. What's holding it back is construction cost — and that's an engineering and regulatory problem, not a fundamental one. As research drives capex down (SMRs, factory-built reactors, advanced fuel cycles), this industry doesn't grow linearly — it unlocks by orders of magnitude. I think nuclear becomes the dominant energy source worldwide, and I want to understand the path there deeply.

**Systems that compound.** Most software is stateless — you use it, it does a thing, it forgets. I build the opposite. In CloutFarm, when a customer does a strategy interview, that conversation becomes knowledge. When they edit a draft, the system learns their voice. When a post performs well, that signal feeds back into how the next one is written. Six months in, the platform knows your expertise, your audience, your tone, what works and what doesn't — and every output reflects that. This is why I care about compounding systems: a product that gets better with use is a product people can't leave. One that resets every session is one they'll replace the moment something shinier shows up.

**Value capture.** In any ecosystem, value gets created at many points — but most of it leaks. The obvious move is to monetize what people are already paying attention to. But the non-obvious move — building for a part of the system no one's capturing yet — is often what unlocks the real bottleneck to growth. Sometimes the most valuable thing you can build isn't a revenue line, it's the piece that makes everything else work. So I build systems that actually capture value — not by charging more, but by owning the layer where value was leaking in the first place.

**Substance over shine.** I'd rather build something that works quietly for years than something that trends for a week. Good software doesn't need a launch event. Good infrastructure doesn't need a blog post. I'm skeptical of anything that requires constant explanation to justify its existence — if it's working, you can tell.
